Visa FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions — USA Tourist Visa

Common questions about USA visa requirements, processing, and our assistance service.

How long does USA tourist visa processing take?
Processing time depends on visa appointment availability and embassy schedule. Interview wait times can range from a few weeks to several months depending on demand. We help you secure your slot as early as possible. Processing times can vary depending on the embassy or consulate workload. We recommend applying at least 4–8 weeks before your intended travel date to allow buffer time.
What documents do I need for a USA tourist visa?
Typical requirements include a valid passport (with at least 6 months validity), filled-out application form, recent ID photos, proof of financial capacity (bank statements, ITR), proof of employment, travel itinerary, and accommodation bookings. We provide a complete document checklist tailored to your situation when you sign up for our service.
What's included in your ₱3,500 assistance fee?
Our all-inclusive fee covers: application form completion, document review, appointment scheduling at the US Embassy, interview coaching (if required), submission coordination, and ongoing support throughout the process. Specific inclusions depend on the country — please see the service breakdown on this page.
Do I need to attend a visa interview?
It depends on the country and your case. For some USA applications, interviews are not always required for tourist visas, but the embassy may request one. If an interview is required, we provide coaching so you go in prepared and confident.
What happens if my visa is denied?
Visa decisions are made solely by the embassy or consulate, and we cannot guarantee approval. However, we maximize your chances by ensuring your documents are complete, properly prepared, and your application is submitted correctly. If you are denied, we provide guidance on possible next steps, including reapplication strategies.
Can you help with multiple-entry visas?
Yes, we can guide you through multiple-entry visa applications where applicable. Eligibility depends on the embassy's policies and your travel history. Talk to our team to learn what options are available for you.
What's the difference between a B1 and B2 visa?
B1 is for business visitors (meetings, conferences, negotiations) and B2 is for tourism, vacation, medical treatment, and visiting family. Most applicants get a combined B1/B2 visa which covers both purposes for up to 10 years (subject to embassy approval).
